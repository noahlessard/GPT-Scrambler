The American Civil War, also known as the War Between the States, was a conflict fought from 1861 to 1865 in the United States between the northern states and the southern states․ The main cause of the war was the disagreement over the issue of slavery, although other issues such as states' rights and regional economic differences also played a role․ The war resulted in the deaths of over 620,000 soldiers and civilians, making it one of the deadliest conflicts in American history․ The Union, made up of the northern states, ultimately emerged victorious and slavery was abolished as a result of the war․ The Civil War had a significant impact on the United States and its legacy is still felt today․
